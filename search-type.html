What type of service do you provide?

Your answer helps us tailor the content of our digital tool to your service. This is important because there are distinctions between the duties that you must demonstrate compliance with. Not sure? Our Regulation Checker tool provides more guidance on these service types and whether any exemptions apply.

If you provide more than one service (for example, two distinct user-to-user services), you must complete a separate risk assessment for each service. If you provide a combined service, you must comply with both the U2U service and search service risk assessment duties.

You must carry out a suitable and sufficient risk assessment and put in place appropriate safety measures to complete with the relevant legal duties.Our tools will help 

A user-to-user service is an online service that allows its users to interact with each other. This includes the ability to generate, upload or share content, such as images, videos, messages or comments, with other users of that online service. User-to-user services include online services which allow private messaging between users. Here, you do not need to consider content that you (the online service provider) publish directly, as it is not relevant for this question.

A search service is an online service which is, or includes, a search engine. A search engine is a feature which enable users to search more than one website and/or database. However, if your online service only searches one website or database, it will not be classed as a search service. For example, where a website has a search bar feature to find content internally on that website. Some online services use or embed a search engine provided by a third party (e.g. as a “plug in”). Where this embedded search engine is not controlled by you but by a third party, the regulations related to search services are likely to apply to the third party.
