Content harmful to children

Non-designated content (NDC)

All service providers completing a children’s risk assessment have a duty to identify and assess the risk of children encountering NDC on their service.

Non-designated content is content “of a kind which presents a material risk of significant harm to an appreciable number of children in the United Kingdom.”

  • ‘Significant harm’ means physical or psychological harm that causes lasting impact on children’s wellbeing, attitudes or behaviour, whether directly to users or indirectly to others
  • ‘Material risk’ related to the likelihood of harm occurring, and this can be assessed by linking significant harm to a specific kind of content
  • ‘Appreciable number’ does not necessarily have to be a very large number of children, but not one child or very few children

In the Children’s Register of Risks, we provide more information on non-designated content, including a framework for identifying kinds of non-designated content , based on the definition set out in the Act. This framework has four steps:

  1. What harms are arising from children viewing online content?
  2. Is the harm identified significant?
  3. Is there a material risk of that harm occurring?
  4. Are an ‘appreciable’ number of children in the UK affected?

Assessment of NDC will vary for different services.

Resources to help you assess the risk of NDC on your service

Children’s Register of Risks

We expect all service providers to consider the framework in the Children’s Register of Risk which includes categories of non-designated content which Ofcom has. You should consider whether any of these may be a risk on your service.

Core evidence inputs

For each kind of content harmful to children, you should consider the types of evidence and any other relevant information you already hold. We’ll explain types of core evidence as you progress through this step.

Risk factors

You may find it useful to consider the risk factors that were identified in Step 1 when assessing NDC. We’ll remind you of these on the next page so you can take note of them if you haven’t already.

Recording and reporting NDC

Where you identify that NDC is present on your service, you must notify Ofcom of the kinds of content identified as NDC and the incidence of such NDC on your service.
