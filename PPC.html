Your recommended safety measures to be compliant

You now need to decide what your service is going to do to reduce the risk of harm to individuals that you have identified.

Based on the information you have provided to us throughout our digital tools, we have provided the following recommended safety measures for your online service from our Codes of Practice.

If you decide to implement measures recommended to you from our Codes of Practice, you will be treated as complying with the relevant duty. This means that Ofcom will not take enforcement action against you for breach of that duty.  

Your recommended safety measures

The harms that we have highlighted are the key types of illegal harm that are most relevant to the risk factors that you identified in Step 1. This will help you prioritise the most relevant parts of our Register of Risk that we encourage services to consult. This provides more detailed information to help you understand how harms may manifest on your service.

Governance and accountability

Annual risk management review

ICU A1

This measure sets out that the most senior governance body should carry out and record an annual review of risk management activities in relation to online safety, and how developing governance risks are being monitored and managed.

You should consult our codes of practice to understand more about this measure.

Relevant codes: Child sexual exploitation and abuse (CSEA), Terrorism, Other duties

Relevant duties : Section 10, 20, 21 Online Safety Act
