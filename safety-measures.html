Additional safety measures

For some, the recommended measures from Ofcom’s codes of practice will not be comprehensive in addressing all the risks of harm that you have identified in your risk assessment. In this case, you may decide that it is necessary to identify additional measures which go beyond the recommended measures to help you to reduce these risks of harm.


Good practice in risk management indicates that organisations are likely to be more effective at prioritising and managing adverse events when they have a good understanding of their residual risk level.

Identify your measures | Alternative safety measures

Alternative safety measures

The measures we have presented to you in this tool are those that Ofcom recommends you take to comply with your duties under the Online Safety Act. However, you do not need to follow these if you already have alternative measures in place to comply.


Nobody knows your service better than you, and you may be well placed to identify effective measures or innovative approaches to prevent harm as an alternative approach to the recommended measures that we have presented to you from our Codes of Practice.

Implement and record | Record your measures

Record your measures

Recommended measures

Where you decide to adopt the measures recommended to you in this tool to comply with the safety duties, you should keep a written record of it. We have created an easy-to-follow list below to help your

  • Provide a description of the measure,
  • Identify the relevant code of practice for the measure, and
  • You should include the date on which the measure takes effect.

    Alternative measures

    If you choose to adopt, or have already adopted, alternative measures then you must make and keep a written record of the alternative measures. We have set out what you must record below:

    • The recommended measures that you have chosen not to use (this does not include measures that do not apply to your service),
    • The alternative measures that have been taken or are in use,
    • How these alternative measures amount to compliance with the duty in question, and
    • How you have complied with right to freedom of expression, and the protection of users privacy,
    • The date the alternative measures came into effect.

    Implement and record | Completing your risk assessment

    Completing your risk assessment

    To make sure that your risk assessment is of the suitable and sufficient standard required by the Online Safety Act, you must make sure you keep a record of your risk assessment which shows:

    • what service the risk assessment applies to
    • the date the risk assessment was completed
    • the date the risk assessment was reviewed or updated, if this applies
    • who completed the risk assessment
    • who had oversight of and/or approved the findings of the risk assessment.

    You should also include the information you have gathered about how you have undertaken the risk assessment, and its findings.

    You can use our template to help you bring this information together to ensure that you meet your record-keeping duties.

    Implement and record | Checklist

    Checklist

    Before you move on

    • You have decided the measures your service is going to implement to reduce the risks of harm to individuals by consulting Ofcom’s Codes of Practice or by considering alternative ways to meet the safety duties.

    • You have considered any additional measures that may be appropriate for your service to put in place that go beyond Ofcom’s Codes of Practice.

    • You have implemented and made a record of these measures including why you think these measures meet the relevant safety duties;

      • You have recorded any measures from Ofcom’s Codes of Practice that have been or are planned to be implemented, and any measures that are not in use.
      • You have recorded any measures that are alternative to those set out in Ofcom’s Codes of Practice, with an explanation how the measures meet the relevant duties.
      • You have made a complete record of your risk assessment, as per Ofcom’s guidance on record keeping and review.
