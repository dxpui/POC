Primary priority content (PPC)

The Online Safety Act sets out a list of the kinds of content that are regarded as primary priority content, as decided by Parliament. All children should be prevented from encountering primary priority content.

We encourage services to consult our Children’s Register of Risk and Guidance on Content Harmful to Children. These provide more detailed information and examples to help you understand what constitutes each kind of content harmful to children, and how it can manifest on your service. For more information and examples to help determine where content may be illegal, you can consult the Illegal Content Judgements Guidance.

Suicide content

This relates to content of such a nature that it is reasonable to assume that it was produced solely or principally for the purpose of sexual arousal. Pornographic content may include artificial images, such as AI-generated images, animated images or videos or paintings, drawings, or other non-photographic images. It does not include content that consists only of text or consists only of text and is accompanied by identifying content which is not itself pornographic.

This content may be illegal in circumstances where it:
·       includes material which meets the definition of extreme pornography
·       involves intimate image abuse
·       involves ‘cyberflashing’
·       constitutes child sexual abuse material
·       is used in the commission of illegal offences.
Pornographic content

This relates to content which encourages, promotes or provides instructions for suicide. This does not need to be intentional, deliberate or explicit to constitute content harmful to children. This includes content which glamourises, glorifies, romanticises or normalises suicide.

This content may be illegal in circumstances where it amounts to an offence of intentionally encouraging or assisting the suicide of another person.
