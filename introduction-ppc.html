Understand the kinds of content harmful to children that needs to be assessed

In your children’s risk assessment, you must assess the level of risk of different types of content harmful to children (CHC) on your service.

You will need to assess the risk of children encountering:

  • 4 kinds of primary priority content (PPC)
  • 8 kinds of priority content (PC)
  • non-designated content[AG1]

Use risk factors to identify content harmful to children on your service

In this step, we will ask you a series of questions about your service.

Based on your answers, we’ll provide you with information about risk factors. Risk factors are functionalities and features you may have on your service, such as private messaging, that can increase the risk of children encountering content harmful to children on your service.

Once you have an idea of which risk factors your service has, you can assess the kinds of content harmful to children related to each risk factor.

Records you need to make in this step

As you progress through this step, you will need to record:

  • confirmation that you have consulted Ofcom’s Children’s Risk Profiles
  • any risk factors that are relevant to your service

You can use our record-keeping template to help you record the information.

How long this step will take to complete

We estimate that this step will take up to an hour to complete but this will vary depending on the size of your organisation and range of services you provide.
